-YUV vs RGB: with rgb, we need 3x of bandwidth

with yuv, we can encode u&v with smaller bandwith without losing a lot on performance

-PAL,SECAM,NTSC modulation: method of encoding RGB signals with limited bandwidth

used in TV broadcast & DVD reader (see figure 2.4 for more detail)

-PAL,SECAM,NTSC format: can be seen as different ways of video display

DVD reader uses MPEG compression and can be display in PAL(Europe) or NTSC(American) format

-In digital component video, the video signals are in digital form (YCbCr or R´G´B´),being encoded to

composite NTSC, PAL, or SECAM only when it is necessary for broadcasting or recording purposes.

-Analog Display (CRT) vs Digital Display (LCD)

most CRT displays are interlaced while LCD,plasma,computer displays are progressive.

1. freq 50Hz: interlaced (not progressive)

-50 scans/second: alternative line odd vs even

-25 frames/second

2. framesize 625x864

-Fs = 25*625*864 = 13.5MHz(sampling clock of PAM)

-line scanning frequency(Fh) = 25*625 = 15.625KHz~64us

-video size: 576x702 (The digital standard resolution is 720x576 pixel for DVDs,

480x576 pixel for SVCDs and 352x288 pixel for VCDs)

-sync size: 49x162

->input Y,U,V : 3 matrix of size 576x702 (active)

->ouput My,Mu,Mv: 3 matrix of size 625x864 (active+sync)
